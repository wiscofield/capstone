Welcome Back to the Future!

  • 31 July 2020
  • San Francisco
  • TAGS: #history, #CSS, #blogging

Here we are in 2020...

At the dawn of a new decade! And, over the past ten years, technology's ubiquity has simply skyrocketed. Nowadays, every man, woman, child and dog is glued to a screen of some sort (and often multiple all at once). And as such circumstances exist, our society has adapted extremely well in a myriad of ways; and tech's growth remains at a near exponential rate. The way we interact and network has changed drastically from what is once was, and the stylistic choices of this page (relative to those before it) should demonstrate exactly this. With people sharing their lives all over social media rather than in their personal blog, you see a very different kind of blog today. Those that I have encountered recently often fit into various niches of popular culture such as food blogs, (this one is my personal favorite) or lifestyle blogs, like Gwenyth Paltrow's brainchild, GOOP. Though, as of late, I have also noticed an influx of personal websites for the professional/portfolio purposes of web designers, photographers(this is my childhood bff!!), young professionals, etc.

From all of these sites, though, I saw threads of common themes... And above all, there seems to have been a near unanimous rejection of any type of box shadow or textured background you may have seen just ten years prior.

Instead, websites today...

  1. take minimalism to the max ! #CleanLinesOnly
  2. have structure! HALLELUJAH! (thanks to CSS grid)
  3. & feature subtlety in design, as evidenced by the plethora of typefaces you might find on one site

While our mission for this capstone project was to uncover as much about CSS as we possibly could within the amount of time we were allotted, we also discovered that there is so much beyond what CSS can do! And now, with our strengthened grasp of I feel that we are well-equipped to explore!

Thank you for joining us in this journey through time!
